2024 Kenya Drone Regulations: What You Need to Know

By Wanjiku Mwangi July 5, 2024
6 min read Share

Kenya's drone industry is booming, but strict KCAA regulations govern all commercial operations. This 2024 guide covers everything from licensing to no-fly zones and recent changes affecting GIS professionals.

Licensing Requirements

As of January 2024, all commercial drone operators must comply with these KCAA requirements:

Remote Pilot License

  • Mandatory for all commercial operators
  • Requires 20 hours of certified training
  • Written exam (75% pass mark)
  • KSh 15,000 application fee

Drone Registration

  • All drones > 250g must be registered
  • KSh 5,000 annual fee per drone
  • Requires proof of ownership
  • Registration number must be displayed

Insurance

  • Minimum KSh 5M liability coverage
  • Must cover third-party risks
  • Approved providers only

No-Fly Zones & Restrictions

Kenya's airspace has strict limitations for drone operations:

No-Fly Zones (Strictly prohibited)
Restricted (Special permission required)
Permitted Areas

Key Restricted Areas

  • 5km radius around all airports
  • Military installations (All bases nationwide)
  • National parks (Except with KWS approval)
  • Nairobi CBD (Daytime flights only with police escort)

2024 Regulation Changes

March 2024

Night Operations Allowed

With enhanced lighting and prior KCAA approval

January 2024

Reduced Processing Times

Applications now processed within 14 working days (down from 21)

November 2023

New Weight Categories

Introduction of Class C (2kg-5kg) with modified requirements

Special Permissions

These operations require additional approvals:

Operation Type Approval Needed Processing Time Fee
Beyond Visual Line of Sight (BVLOS) KCAA + Air Traffic Control 21 days KSh 25,000
Night Operations KCAA + Local Police 14 days KSh 10,000
National Parks KCAA + Kenya Wildlife Service 30 days KSh 50,000
Urban Areas (Nairobi/Mombasa) KCAA + County Government 10 days KSh 15,000

Pre-Flight Compliance Checklist

Documentation

  • Valid Remote Pilot License
  • Drone Certificate of Registration
  • Insurance Certificate
  • Flight Approval (if applicable)

Equipment

  • Functioning GPS
  • Return-to-home capability
  • Anti-collision lights
  • Fire extinguisher (for drones > 2kg)

Operational

  • Weather assessment
  • NOTAMs checked
  • Emergency contacts available
  • Visual observer (if required)

Penalties for Non-Compliance

Minor Violations

Flying without registration

KSh 50,000 fine

Moderate Violations

Operating in restricted zones

KSh 200,000 fine

Severe Violations

Endangering aircraft

3+ years imprisonment
